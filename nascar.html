The Origins

Before NASCAR became a thing, during prohibition, bootleggers
started modifying cars to be able to escape the police. They would
modify the engine to be more powerful, take out the passenger
and rear seats to store as much liquor as possible, add more
suspension springs to handle the extra weight, and put a dirt
protecting plate in front of the radiator.These cars would remain
looking almost stock on the outside. These people would transport
mass amounts of liquor to hidden bars behind normal businesses,
and if they were caught, they had no problem running.

When prohibition ended, the bootleggers still wanted a way to feel
the adrenaline of driving as fast as they could.

The Series

In 1948, Bill France Sr. founded the National Association for Stock
Car Auto Racing (NASCAR) and held the very first race on Daytona
Beach, Florida February 15, 1948.

The rules stated that the cars had to be strictly “stock”, right off the
showroom floor. The only addition that could be made was a paint job
to signify the team.

How The Cars Evolved

At first, the cars started to get heavier but with bigger engines. In the
50s, the cars started getting lighter and more sporty, engines kept
getting bigger, some cars started reaching 200mph. The newer cars
became less and less like stock cars and more like a roll cage with a
body. The cars started getting so fast that it became a safety hazard, so
rules started regulating the speed.

What It Is Today

Modern nascars are almost entirely roll cage, keeping safety over
everything else. They have a thin flexible body that allows the inner
parts of the car to do its job in the event of a crash, they also have rear
facing roof flaps to try and keep the car on the ground if it were to
spin out. These cars are designed specifically with aerodynamics
in mind.

Types Of Tracks

There is more to NASCAR than just driving in a circle, sure thats most
of it but there are other types of tracks, like Circuit of the Americas and
many more, every track is different and they all have their own
challenges as well.

Types Of Cars

There is also more than just cars. The cup series, and xfinity series are
the most popular, but there is also the craftsman truck series that adds
a new tier of car to the list, and a unique one at that.

In Conclusion

NASCAR has more to it than most people realise and the history behind
it is very interesting.
